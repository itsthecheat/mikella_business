Coaching and Bodywork

Motivational Coaching

Coaching offers an alternative to psychotherapy that is goal oriented, results driven, and short term. This offering is well-suited to those who do not wish to focus on unconscious conflict, and prefer to seek present time solutions for current problems. In my practice of coaching, strong emphasis is placed on commitments to take action between sessions and is intended to be fast moving. We will work together to identify areas in your life that need adjustment, clarify future goals, and develop a plan of action. Coaching can be remotely or in person.

Dreamwork

Mikella's approach to dreamwork is drawn from the gestalt style, during which a dream is explored using present tense, first person recreation of the dream space. Dreamwork allows for the practitioner and client to coexist in the dream space and explore it from different perspectives, often leading the client to new insights and resolution of unconscious conflicts. This style differs from dream analysis in its focus on active exploration, rather than interpretation. Dreamwork can be utilized in conjunction with psychotherapy or as a standalone practice.

Breathwork

Breathwork is a practice that uses a two-part inhalation to rapidly and deeply oxygenate the body, activating the nervous system and allowing for somatic release of stored trauma. It can sometimes produce strong emotional reactions concurrent with physical sensations, and works quickly to provide relief and healing from trauma and pain. Frequently one may also experience altered states of consciousness that can provide clarity and insight into both blocks and paths forward. It can also be a tool for enhancing creative process and cultivating intuition. Mikella was inspired to include this in her practice through her personal use of breathwork, and the observation of its many benefits. Her extensive experience as a trauma-focused mental health provider allows for sensitive and skilled facilitation of breathwork for individuals with complex trauma histories. Breathwork is offered as a standalone treatment, and can also be incorporated into psychotherapy if desired.

Ericksonian Hypnosis

This style of hypnosis is ultimately a self-healing practice, wherein the hypnotist is a catalyst for the individual to learn the skill of exploring their unconscious mind, and using that process to tap into inner resources, groundedness, creative flow, and increased focus. Hypnosis can be used for a variety of purposes, including changing habits, resolving unconscious blocks and rigidity, and relaxation, and does not require any previous experience. It can be used alone, or combined with other modalities and is a supportive tool to enhance meditation practice.

Reiki


Reiki is translated as Universal Lifeforce Energy, and is a gentle and supportive form of energy work. It be used in conjunction with any of the aforementioned services.

Please note that Breathwork, Reiki, and Coaching are not a substitute for Psychotherapy or treatment for medical illness. If you have concerns about whether you can safely practice Breathwork or receive Reiki please consult your doctor.

About

In 2018 Mikella trained with Erin Telford to facilitate 2-stage Pranayama breathwork meditation (in the style of David Elliot) as a somatic approach to supporting trauma processing. She is also a level two certified reiki practitioner, and was attuned by Shannon O'Neil-Loyola of Radical Reiki. As an expansion to her training in working with unconscious and trance states, Mikella has completed her Level 1 Certificate in Ericsonian Hypnosis in 2019. Combined with her skills as a psychotherapist and meditation practitioner she uses these techniques to help individuals connect with their unconscious mind, work through blocks, and achieve a greater sense of embodiment and relaxation.
